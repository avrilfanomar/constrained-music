Below is a **structured, compact map of commonly taught “rules” in Western music**.
They are **descriptive conventions**, not laws—regularly bent or broken by composers.

---

## I. Core Structural & Notational Conventions (Pan-Western)

### Meter & Rhythm

* **Meter consistency**: beats grouped regularly (duple/triple/quadruple).
* **Strong/weak beat hierarchy**.
* **Anacrusis (pickup)**: piece may start before beat 1; final bar compensates.
* **Barline alignment**: harmonic rhythm usually aligns with strong beats.
* **Syncopation**: emphasis on weak beats is marked, not default.
* **Tempo stability**: changes are indicated explicitly (rit., accel.).

### Pitch & Scales

* **Octave equivalence**: pitches repeat every octave.
* **12-TET system** (modern standard).
* **Diatonic scales** as default (major/minor).
* **Accidentals are local** (apply within the bar unless tied).

### Notation

* **Clefs define pitch space**.
* **Key signatures define default accidentals**.
* **Ties = same pitch, slurs = phrasing/articulation**.
* **Dots add 50% duration**.
* **Tuplets subdivide evenly against the meter**.

---

## II. Tonal Harmony Rules (Common Practice Period)

### Tonality

* **Tonal center (tonic)** governs hierarchy.
* **Functional harmony**: Tonic → Predominant → Dominant → Tonic.
* **Cadences mark closure** (authentic, half, plagal, deceptive).

### Chord Behavior

* **Triads built in thirds**.
* **Dissonance requires preparation & resolution**.
* **Dominant (V) resolves to tonic (I)**.
* **Leading tone resolves upward**.
* **Seventh chords resolve by step**.

### Modulation

* **Key changes via pivot chords or dominant preparation**.
* **Closely related keys preferred** (classical norm).

---

## III. Voice Leading Rules (Strictest “Rules”)

### Motion

* **Prefer stepwise motion** over leaps.
* **Large leaps compensated by step in opposite direction**.
* **Contrary motion preferred** between voices.

### Intervals

* **Avoid parallel perfect fifths & octaves**.
* **Avoid voice crossing**.
* **Maintain voice independence**.
* **Outer voices define harmonic clarity**.

---

## IV. Melody & Phrase Structure

* **Motivic coherence** (ideas repeat/transform).
* **Balanced phrases** (often 2+2 or 4+4 bars).
* **Climax placed late, not at start**.
* **Range limits** (melodies sit in singable registers).
* **Ending on tonic for closure**.

---

## V. Form & Large-Scale Structure

* **Binary (AB), Ternary (ABA), Rondo (ABACA), Sonata form**.
* **Contrast + return** is foundational.
* **Development = instability, recapitulation = resolution**.
* **Final cadence confirms tonic**.

---

## VI. Texture & Counterpoint

* **Monophony, homophony, polyphony** are distinct textures.
* **Imitation follows intervallic logic**.
* **Counterpoint prioritizes linear logic over chords**.
* **No unresolved dissonance in strict styles**.

---

## VII. Tuning & Timbre

* **Consonance hierarchy** (octave > fifth > third).
* **Equal temperament compromises purity for flexibility**.
* **Instrument ranges respected**.
* **Idiomatic writing** (what the instrument does well).

---

# Genre-Related Rules (Contextual, Not Universal)

## Classical / Baroque / Common Practice

* Strict voice leading
* Functional harmony
* Clear formal symmetry
* Ornamentation follows period rules

## Romantic

* Expanded harmony
* Chromaticism allowed
* Rules bent for expression
* Longer phrases, delayed resolution

## Jazz

* **Extended chords (7ths, 9ths, 13ths)** are default
* **ii–V–I progressions**
* **Swing feel**
* **Improvisation over changes**
* Parallel fifths allowed
* Dissonance embraced

## Blues

* **12-bar form**
* **Blues scale (♭3, ♭5, ♭7)**
* Dominant chords on all degrees
* Call-and-response phrasing

## Pop / Rock

* **Looped chord progressions**
* **Hook-centric structure**
* Simpler harmony, strong groove
* Studio production as composition element

## Folk / Traditional

* Modal scales
* Repetitive forms
* Oral-tradition flexibility
* Rhythm tied to dance or work

## Contemporary / Modernist

* Atonality or post-tonality
* Serialism or free pitch
* Rhythm detached from meter
* Rules replaced by systems

---

## Meta-Rule (Most Important)

> **Every “rule” is style-dependent, historically bounded, and optional once understood.**
